Paper Abstract

Abstract

Time-series analysis underpins decision making across domains such as economics, meteorology, and public health, and the field has recently shifted from unimodal time series toward multimodal formulations that pair temporal signals with textual context. Most existing work, however, either omits the visual modality or renders it only as a chart-style visualization of the same signal, rather than treating it as an independent evidence source. Real-world tasks instead depend on three non-redundant evidence sources at once: vision encodes physical and semantic context, text supplies events and instructions, and time series carries high-resolution quantitative dynamics.

To address this gap, MMTA (Multimodal Temporal Analysis) is introduced as a real-world benchmark that places vision, text, and time series side by side. MMTA spans nine domains -- robotics, autonomous driving, human action, manufacturing, AR assistance, healthcare, recommendation, finance, and climate -- exported under video+TS and image+TS layouts, with a 3,524-sample test split covering both classification and forecasting. A systematic evaluation of eight MLLMs on MMTA exposes a shared failure mode: serializing time series as text yields excessively long token sequences and prevents alignment with co-occurring video frames. We then introduce OmniTS, a working example of how an Omni-LLM can be extended to handle long time series, which demonstrates higher efficiency on the time-series modality, competitive task performance, and tighter fusion across vision, text, and time series.
